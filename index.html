About the Romans
and Their History

The Romans, an ancient civilisation from 753 BC, formed a powerful empire lasting centuries. Western Rome fell in 476 AD, and the eastern part in 1453 AD.

Originating in present-day Italy as a small city-state named Rome, they expanded through military conquests, becoming highly influential. Their rich culture, sophisticated government, inventions, and lasting impact on art, architecture, and language mark a significant chapter in human civilisation.

The Roman Goverment

The Roman government evolved from kingship to a Republic in 509 BC, with a powerful Senate and two annual consuls. Eventually, it became an Empire under emperors, starting with Augustus in 27 BC. The emperors held absolute authority as both political and military leaders, shaping the course of Western civilization.

For more about Rome's governance, click on the title to learn more.

The Roman Inventions

The Romans were ingenious inventors, contributing groundbreaking innovations to human civilisation.

They engineered the arch, dome, aqueducts for fresh water, and durable concrete, revolutionising architecture and construction.

Their advanced road systems facilitated efficient transportation and communication, leaving a lasting legacy in the modern world.

For more about Rome's inventions, click on the title to learn more.

The Roman Culture

Roman culture was a vibrant blend of diverse influences, including art, literature, architecture, and grand festivals like gladiator fights and chariot races at the Colosseum.

Their religious beliefs, social gatherings, and contributions to law and language have left a lasting impact on the world.
